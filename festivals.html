Songkran: The Water Festival

Songkran is the Thai New Year's national holiday. Songkran is on the 13 April every year, but the holiday period extends from 14 to 15 April. In 2018 the Thai cabinet extended the festival nationwide to five days, 12â€“16 April, to enable citizens to travel home for the holiday.

The holiday is known for its water festivals which are mostly celebrated by young people. Major streets are closed to traffic, and are used as arenas for water fights. Celebrants, young and old, participate in this tradition by splashing water on each other.

Traditional water pouring is meant as a symbol of washing away all of one's sins and bad luck and is sometimes filled with fragrant herbs when celebrated in the traditional manner.
